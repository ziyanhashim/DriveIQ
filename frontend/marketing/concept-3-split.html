QUESTIONS

Frequently asked questions

DriveIQ uses your phone's accelerometer, gyroscope, and GPS sensors to capture driving data at 10Hz. This data flows through our 4-phase ML pipeline: preprocessing, LSTM neural network classification, KNN anomaly scoring, and fuzzy logic recommendations -- giving you a comprehensive view of your driving behavior in every session.
We collect GPS coordinates, accelerometer readings (X, Y, Z axes), gyroscope data, speed, and bearing from your device's sensors during active driving sessions. We extract 39 engineered features from this raw data for analysis. No audio, video, or personal browsing data is ever collected.
Absolutely. All data is encrypted in transit and at rest. We use MongoDB Atlas with enterprise-grade security, JWT-based authentication, and bcrypt password hashing. Your driving data is only accessible to you and, if you choose, your assigned instructor.
Only if you are enrolled with an instructor through our booking system. Instructors have role-based access that lets them view session reports and scores for their assigned students. They cannot access data from students who are not booked with them.
Our LSTM models are trained on labeled driving datasets with road-type-specific architectures (motor and secondary road models). The system uses 4-minute analysis windows with 39 features per window, achieving high classification accuracy for normal, aggressive, and drowsy driving patterns. KNN anomaly detection adds an extra layer of validation to reduce false positives.
